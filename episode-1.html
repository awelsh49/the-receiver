The Night It Appeared

Brody Johnson is twenty-seven. He works late shifts at a distribution warehouse on the edge of Alder Reach, a small town in northern Ontario. Most people call it industrial. Brody calls it forgettable.

The town sits beneath a web of towers and lines that hum day and night. Microwave dishes. Power stations. Fiber hubs. Infrastructure people drive past without thinking about. It’s not a place you visit. It’s a place you live your life in. Quiet. Cold. Predictable.

On a Thursday in early October, Brody stepped onto his back deck during a break. He was taking a picture of the rain coming down Harding Street.

That’s when he saw it.

In the phone’s screen, something appeared in the frame that wasn’t on the deck.

It looked like a piece of machinery — part lamp, part signal, part old instrument. Dark metal. Glass.

It stayed there when he moved the phone. Not in the world exactly. In the frame.

Brody doesn’t know what it is. But it feels like it’s trying to communicate.

A recorded signal remains.

What does the signal mean? Is it trying to communicate something? Look closely. If it is, answer through the Receiver.
